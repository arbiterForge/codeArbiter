# Contributor License Agreement (Template)

> **This is a template, not legal advice.** It has not been reviewed by an attorney and is not yet in
> force. Have it reviewed by qualified legal counsel before relying on it or asking contributors to
> sign it. Until then it documents intent only.

## Why this exists

codeArbiter is dual-licensed. It is offered to the public under the GNU AGPLv3 (see
[LICENSE](./LICENSE)), and the copyright holder also offers it under separate proprietary terms (see
the "Dual-Licensing & Contributions" section of the [README](./README.md)). For that model to hold,
the copyright holder must be able to license every line of the project under both sets of terms. This
agreement lets contributors keep ownership of their work while granting the copyright holder the
rights needed to relicense it.

to the following with the project's copyright holder ("the Owner", currently SUaDtL).

royalty-free, irrevocable copyright license to reproduce, prepare derivative works of, publicly
display, sublicense, and distribute Your Contribution and such derivative works, **under both the
AGPLv3 and under separate proprietary or commercial terms of the Owner's choosing**.

### 3. Patent license

You grant the Owner a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free, irrevocable patent license to
make, use, sell, offer to sell, import, and otherwise transfer Your Contribution. This license covers

by the combination of Your Contribution with the project.

### 4. Your representations

You represent that:

- Each Contribution is Your original creation, or You have the right to submit it under this agreement.
- If Your employer has rights to intellectual property You create, You have received permission to
  make the Contribution on behalf of that employer, or the employer has waived those rights for the
  Contribution.
- To Your knowledge, Your Contribution does not violate any third party's rights.

### 5. No obligation

The Owner is not obligated to use or include Your Contribution. This agreement creates no employment,
partnership, or agency relationship.

### 6. No warranty

Except for the representations in Section 4, Your Contribution is provided "as is", without warranty of
any kind.
